1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit, such as your preferred language and other settings, which can make your next visit easier and the site more useful to you.

We also use similar technologies such as pixel tags, web beacons, and local storage that function in a similar way to cookies.

3. How to Control Cookies

You have several options to control or limit how we and our partners use cookies:

  • Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse new cookies, accept cookies only from specific sites, and delete existing cookies. Check your browser's help documentation.
  • Google Ads Settings: www.google.com/settings/ads — opt out of personalized ads
  • Google Analytics Opt-out: tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout
  • Digital Advertising Alliance: aboutads.info/choices
  • Network Advertising Initiative: networkadvertising.org/choices
  • Your Online Choices (EU): youronlinechoices.eu

⚠️ Note: Disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website. Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for the site to function.

4. Local Storage

In addition to cookies, we use your browser's local storage to save your preferences (such as language selection). Local storage data does not expire automatically and remains until cleared. You can clear local storage through your browser settings.

5. Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that signals to websites that you do not want your online activities tracked. We currently do not respond to "Do Not Track" signals. However, you can use the opt-out options listed above.

6. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top indicates when it was last revised. Continued use of our website after changes constitutes acceptance.
